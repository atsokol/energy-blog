# LinkedIn Post Style Guide

Use this guide when generating a LinkedIn post for a blog article.

## Tone
- Analytical and direct — written for energy professionals, investors, and policy people
- No jargon without explanation; no buzzwords 
- No hedging ("it seems", "perhaps", "might be") — make the claim, then show the evidence
- Uses em-dash (—) to attach interpretation to a fact: "prices move in sync — indicating strong regional linkages"
- Parenthetical qualifications for secondary detail: "(300k+ incl. PHEVs)", "(with wide seasonal variation)"
- Present participles for analytical framing: "indicating", "signalling", "highlighting"
- Transition words accepted when earned: "Notably,"

## Voice
- First-person possessive "my" allowed ("My next post in my #InfraEnergyData series…"). Vary the wording; don't repeat the same CTA across posts.
- Otherwise: declarative third-person or impersonal constructions — avoid "I think", "I found", etc.

## Length
- 200–250 words (excluding the CTA line)
- Every sentence earns its place

## Structure

1. **Series opener** (1 sentence) — always frames the post as part of the series:
Vary the wording; don't repeat the same CTA across posts.

2. **Key takeaways / Key observations** — a labelled list of findings:
   - Header: `Key takeaways:` or `Key observations:` (pick whichever fits)
   - 3–5 items; concrete numbers where possible
   - Plain bullets (denser, more technical posts)  with no emoji, full sentences

3. **CTA** (1 sentence + link cue) — short, specific, ends with 👇:
   - `Check out live dashboard for further details. Link in the first comment 👇`
   - `Full interactive report is available via the link in the first comment 👇`
   - Vary the wording; don't repeat the same CTA across posts.

## Link placement
- **Never paste the URL in the post body.** LinkedIn's algorithm penalises outbound links.
- Always end with: `Link in the first comment 👇`
- The 👇 emoji is the only emoji permitted outside of bullet formatting.

## Hashtags
- `#InfraEnergyData` appears inline in the series opener or at the end — that is the primary tag.
- Add 3–5 topic hashtags **at the end of the post** on a separate line if needed (e.g. `#Ukraine #EnergyMarkets #RenewableEnergy`), but they are optional — the posts themselves do not always include a hashtag block.
- Do not stuff hashtags mid-prose beyond the series tag.

## What to avoid
- Vague claims without numbers or evidence
- Repeating the title verbatim as the opener
- Pasting a URL in the post body
- Mentioning interactive controls or data methodology (those belong in the post itself)
- Overly promotional language or calls to "like and share"
